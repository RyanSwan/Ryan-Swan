About Me

Biography

From my formative years as a secondary school student, my inclination towards analytical and business disciplines was clear. With a keen interest in subjects like applied mathematics, accounting, economics, and mathematics, I laid a solid foundation for my academic pursuits, honing my ability to analyse and strategize. These subjects, particularly for my Leaving Certificate, were stepping stones towards understanding my analytical mindset.

When the time came to step into higher education, my analytical acumen was a guiding light, steering me towards a field where I could blend analytical aptitude with strategic thinking. Despite this clarity, choosing a definitive career path had a wide range of possibilities I wasn't ready to narrow down. I opted for Business Studies, a choice that helped me to keep a broad perspective, ensuring my educational journey was not limited to one field.

It was within the vast expanse of Business Studies that I discovered a resonance with Data Analytics. This specialisation felt like a natural alignment with my skills and preferences. The practical, hands-on nature of data analytics, where trends are discovered and decisions are informed by data, appealed to me far more than the abstract world of essay writing. It was a domain where I could see patterns and influence decisions grounded in concrete analysis.

Now, as I navigate my future, the path I want to follow has become clear. I aspire to delve deeper into the realm of Data Analytics through a 2-year graduate program, where I can further refine my skills and contribute to this dynamic field. My journey through secondary school's analytical rigor to my university's specialisation has been a testament to my dedication to this discipline. In pursuing a career in data analytics, I am not just following a profession; I am embracing who I am as a person, and following a career path I believe is suited to my nature. I am eager to explore the vast potentials of data and transform them into actionable insights that drive progress.
