Python Exam

Date: 1st November 2023

Had my first exam on the semester and I have to say it was an incredible experience. Learning a coding language is something that I have wanted to do for a long time now and the first six weeks of this semester has been a grind that I have taken in my stride. The exam consisted of the basics of python (input function was the first question), and working with libraries such as numpy and pandas. I do like numpy, however I think pandas is far more gripping and challenging. Manipulating dataframes is something I really find myself interested in. I think I did pretty well on the exam, although there was a functions question that I struggled with. Overall, I'm really happy with how the data analytics course is going so far!

Machine Learning Exam

Date: 29th November 2023

Today marked the second exam of the semester, this time it was for the MT413 module: 'Data Mining and Predictive Analytics'. This exam was composed of the various elements we have studied for the past two months, such as data cleaning, feature engineering, and the implementation of machine learning algorithms. At first, learning about the theory of these topics was quite intimidating to me, as they seemed to be very high level. However, I could not be happier with how that exam went! I'm very happy with how I cleaned the data, I knew how to implement dummy variables and an encoding dictionary, and my models had a high accuracy measure. Another step in the right direction!

Microsoft Experience

Date: 19th February 2024

Today marked a pivotal moment in our Business Analytics journey — our class had the privilege of attending an exclusive Power BI workshop hosted at Microsoft's headquarters, kindly organised my my classmate, Dami. It wasn't just another day of lectures; it was an immersive experience that introduced us to the transformative capabilities of data visualization and business intelligence.

As we settled into the conference room, the vibrant energy was palpable. Our instructors for the day walked us through the intricacies of Power BI's interface, showcasing how this powerful tool could turn complex datasets into insightful, interactive dashboards. Applying Elon Musk’s 5-Step Design Process to Business Analytics

Elon Musk's 5-step design process, used at SpaceX, streamlines innovation and efficiency. It starts with refining requirements to ensure they're smart and owned by individuals, not departments. Then, it involves critically evaluating parts and processes to eliminate non-value adding elements. The third step is to simplify or optimize only after the first two steps have been executed. Accelerating the cycle time by focusing on one task is the fourth step, ensuring rapid execution of the right tasks. Finally, once the process is streamlined and optimized, it should be automated. This methodical approach encourages focusing on value and fast-paced innovation.

Business Analytics: Essential Competencies for Success

Business analysts are critical thinkers who drive transformation and efficiency by harnessing the power of data to make insightful decisions. Effective communication is key; they must translate complex data into understandable information. Strong analytical skills enable them to decipher data patterns, while tech savviness ensures they can harness modern tools to enhance efficiency. Balancing project management and resourcefulness, business analysts solve problems creatively and maintain project momentum. Emotional intelligence is vital for teamwork, while adaptability and stakeholder management ensure they can handle change and align diverse interests. As lifelong learners, they must continually update their knowledge and skills. Lastly, strategic thinking is essential to align their work with the business's larger goals. Each competency is a vital piece in the complex puzzle of business analytics.
