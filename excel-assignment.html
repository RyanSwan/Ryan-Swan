Flight Overbooking Analysis Project

The project aims to evaluate the economic impact of overbooking strategies for Green Airways' flights from Dublin to London, optimizing for profit while minimizing the cost of compensating bumped passengers. Green Airways operates 3 daily flights on the Dublin to London route, each with a capacity of 100 passengers. Overbooking is a common practice to compensate for no-shows, but it comes with its own risks and costs.

Part A - Basic Model

Utilizing past 12-month data from Data Sheet A, we assessed the impact of overbooking by 1 to 5 passengers on profit margins. Descriptive statistics measured the effect of each overbooking strategy, and a sensitivity analysis was performed to understand the influence of fixed parameters on the model's outcomes.

Part B - Simulation

The model from Part A was refined using Data Sheet B to simulate more complex scenarios, including different compensation schemes for bumped passengers on 11am, 3pm, and 7pm flights. The demand for each flight was modeled using a normal distribution based on specified mean and standard deviations.

Approach and Outcome

Approach

The analysis involved simulating 500 days of operation, testing various overbooking strategies, and conducting a sensitivity analysis to pinpoint the robustness of the model. Assumptions were outlined, and limitations were discussed to suggest potential improvements for realism.

Outcome

This project demonstrated the delicate balance required in overbooking decisions and provided Green Airways with a data-driven approach to maximize profits while enhancing customer satisfaction.
